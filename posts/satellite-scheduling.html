Research Note

How I Think About Dynamic Satellite Mission Scheduling

Opening note · Update this file with your own text

Dynamic Earth observation satellite mission scheduling asks a practical question: when many observation requests arrive over time, how should a satellite choose targets, imaging windows, and attitude maneuvers under physical and operational constraints?

This blog will collect short notes on scheduling models, reinforcement learning methods, benchmark datasets, experiment design, and remote sensing applications. It can also host summaries of new papers, reproducible code, and dataset updates.

Possible post series

  • Dynamic mission scheduling formulations and constraints.
  • Meta reinforcement learning for fast adaptation.
  • Energy-aware SAR regional mapping and multi-strip stitching.
  • Benchmark datasets for Earth-observation scheduling.
