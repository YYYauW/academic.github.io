About me

Welcome. I am a PhD student at the State Key Laboratory of Information Engineering in Surveying, Mapping and Remote Sensing (LIESMARS), Wuhan University.

My research focuses on intelligent optimization for Earth observation satellite mission scheduling. I am interested in operations research, reinforcement learning, spiking neural networks, SAR regional mapping, and spatio-temporal mobility analysis.

This website collects my publications, research notes, and academic links. The current publication list is based on publicly visible information from my ResearchGate profile.

Research

  • Earth observation satellite scheduling. Dynamic mission assignment, imaging window selection, and multi-point observation planning.
  • Learning-based optimization. Meta reinforcement learning, proximal policy optimization, and spiking neural networks for scheduling decisions.
  • SAR regional mapping. Multi-strip stitching, energy-aware imaging, and radiometric consistency correction.
  • Spatio-temporal mobility analysis. Taxi trajectory data, ride-hailing collaboration areas, and urban travel flow stability.
